Earliest Stage in Galaxy Evolution

This project focuses on understanding how galaxies form and evolve in the earliest stages of the universe.

Overview

We know dust temperatures should affect the light radiated by a galaxy. This is reflected in its initial mass function (IMF) which describes the relative density vs mass in the galaxy. For years, it was just assumed that we could just use the Milky Way's IMF. However, our new approach fits IMFs of different temperatures to the COSMOS2020 photometry to get better representations of galaxies and possibly even measure dust temperatures.

Key Objectives

  • Identify and characterize the most distant and earliest galaxies
  • Study the properties of the first stellar populations
  • Understand the role of dust and gas in early galaxy development
  • Investigate the impact of the initial mass function on galaxy evolution

Methods

This research employs advanced photometry analysis techniques on deep field surveys to identify high-redshift galaxies. We apply temperature-dependent Initial Mass Function (IMF) models to better constrain the physical properties of these distant systems.
